- **`DROPCAP_PARAGRAPH_ATOMICITY`** ŌĆö accepted disclosed tech debt (CTO decision 2026-07-21, same status as its cousin `LIST_SPLITTING_ACROSS_PAGES` above). A paragraph carrying a drop cap is excluded from Phase B line-splitting (`LayoutEngine.ts:222`, deliberate ŌĆö a drop cap's first lines are typographically special), so a drop-cap paragraph that does not fit the remaining page space cannot split: it moves whole and, if the page cannot hold it, produces a reconciliation page. **Not corrected now, and correcting it is not planned.**

  **The justification is STRUCTURAL, not about the drop cap's height.** The exclusion makes the *whole paragraph* atomic regardless of what the glyph costs; a long drop-cap paragraph overflows for exactly the reason a long list item does ŌĆö **it cannot be split, full stop**. This is independent of pricing, and deliberately so: the debt survives whatever the drop cap costs. **(Corrected 2026-07-21 ŌĆö do not restore the earlier wording.)** An earlier version of this entry argued the exclusion was harmless because "a drop cap consumes zero extra height (25 ŌåÆ 25 pages, 0 ŌåÆ 0 reconciliations), so there is nothing to charge and the CTO removed pricing from scope." That measurement was subsequently shown to be **the signature of `DROPCAP_TEXT_OVERLAP`, not evidence of no cost** ŌĆö PDFKit overlapped the lines instead of growing them, while Word grows 3 ŌåÆ 4 pages on the same content. Pricing was consequently **reinstated** (the reversal in `docs/DECISIONS.md`) and now ships on `main` (`LayoutEngine.priceDropCapParagraph`): a drop cap *does* carry a real, charged height cost. The atomicity debt stands anyway ŌĆö not because the glyph is free, but because an unsplittable paragraph can overflow no matter how precisely its height is charged.

  **The CTO's condition: the debt may stay uncorrected but may not stay unmeasured.** The obligation is a test proving the property ŌĆö a drop-cap paragraph too long for the page remainder yields a *measured reconciliation*, never a silent overflow ŌĆö the letter of ADR-0051: loud, not silent. **This test is still owed:** the overlap fix's own test (`dropCapOverlap.test.ts`) asserts `unplannedPageBreaks === 0` on a page that *fits*, which is the pricing property, not this atomicity property; no test yet forces a drop-cap paragraph to overflow a page remainder. Also a **coverage gap**: zero `footnoteReference` in any fixture repo-wide (measured), and no fixture is fabricated to fill it.
- **DOCX hyperlinks: external URLs only; internal anchors (cross-references) are unimplemented** (surfaced by chantier B, CTO-confirmed out of scope 2026-07-21). `DOCXRenderer` emits `ExternalHyperlink` for `TypeRun.linkUrl`; there is no bookmark/anchor machinery. `verify-publication-quality` ┬¦4.7 verifies external targets and discloses internal anchors as out of scope. **Coverage gap**: zero `w:hyperlink` in any fixture repo-wide (measured); no fixture fabricated.

- DOCX `RunningHead.content:'chapterTitle'` and `Chapter.startPageNumber` have no real per-chapter effect in `.docx` output (Sprint 6) ŌĆö `DOCXRenderer` builds one Word section for the whole document; true per-chapter behavior needs splitting into multiple sections, not built this sprint.
