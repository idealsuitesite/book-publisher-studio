# Book Publisher Studio — Design Manifesto

*Not a review. Not a spec. The promise every pixel answers to.*

---

**Book Publisher Studio must feel like an editorial workshop, a publishing house, a writer's desk — never a computer terminal.**

We are building the room where someone finishes their book. Rooms like that have warm light and good paper. They have tools within reach that stay where you left them. They are quiet until you need them, and precise the instant you do. Nobody who works in such a room all day thinks about the room — and that is the highest compliment a room can earn.

## The vows

**1. The book is the master of the house.**
Every zone, panel and word exists in service of one author's book. When the studio speaks about the book, it speaks the book's own language — down to rendering its title in the book's own typeface. The tool orbits the work, never the reverse.

**2. Paper, ink, and light — not pixels, borders, and gray.**
Our surfaces are warm like paper, our text is ink, our depth is sheets on a desk — not glass, not neon, not the flat gray of an engineering console. A person should be able to tell from a single screenshot that this software is about *books*.

**3. Show the craft.**
This studio measures real pages, embeds real fonts, splits paragraphs the way typesetters do, and validates against Amazon's actual rules. Powerful engines earn trust by being visible. We surface measured truth everywhere — and we never decorate with numbers we didn't measure.

**4. Every pixel works, and every word is honest.**
No voids dressed as minimalism; no fake data dressed as features. An empty state tells the truth beautifully. A failure report is a gift to the author, delivered with respect and a way forward.

**5. Calm hands, quick tools.**
The studio is quiet: five visual weights, one accent, restrained motion that narrates rather than performs. And it is fast under expert hands: every daily action has a key, every object has a palette entry, and eight hours here should tire the author's mind less than one hour anywhere else.

**6. Guidance, never a straitjacket.**
Status shows the way; sequence never blocks it. The studio suggests the next action because it knows the book — it never forbids a detour, because it respects the author.

---

*Adopted 2026-07-19. Every design review, screen, component and sentence of interface copy is measured against these six vows. When a decision contradicts a vow, the decision is wrong or the vow must be amended here, in writing — never silently.*
